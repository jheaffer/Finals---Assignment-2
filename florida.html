Florida

Florida is a southeastern U.S. state known as the "Sunshine State." It is a peninsula bordered by the Atlantic Ocean and the Gulf of Mexico, famous for its warm climate, wide beaches, major theme parks (including Walt Disney World and Universal), and vibrant cities like Miami, Tampa, and Orlando.

Florida is the only place in the world where both American alligators and American crocodiles live in the wild. It's also home to the Kennedy Space Center, and annually attracts millions of visitors to its beaches and theme parks.

Climate

Much of Florida has a humid subtropical climate (north and central) and a true tropical climate in the southern tip. Summers are hot and humid with frequent thunderstorms; winters are mild and dry in many areas. The state is also susceptible to tropical storms and hurricanes, especially from June through November.
